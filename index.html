The Ballad of Lance

Lance Woolie, originally from Baton Rouge, Louisiana, has navigated a path filled with profound challenges and remarkable achievements. His journey through music, adversity, and personal growth shapes his unique sound and heartfelt storytelling.

Early Life and Struggles

Born on July 3, 1979, Lance’s childhood was marked by familial turmoil and personal loss. The tragic death of his brother, Leigh Michael, in 1995, and subsequent battles with addiction and depression, cast a long shadow over his formative years. His tumultuous youth included stints at multiple schools and a significant period in juvenile detention, where a chance performance with the Angola Travelin’ band sparked his lifelong passion for music.

Musical Beginnings

Lance’s early exposure to music in a correctional facility led to opportunities to lead worship and eventually work outside the prison in the canine unit. These experiences not only shaped his musical talents but also his personal resolve and spiritual depth. After his release, he pursued higher education at Southern University and Louisiana State University, focusing on Information Systems and Decision Sciences.

Diverse Career

Lance’s career took him from tech administration in hospitals to working in South America, where he lived for several years, immersing himself in the local culture and music scene. In Colombia, he released a Christian crossover record, “Let It Ride,” and honed his craft in various musical genres alongside local musicians.

Recent Years and Personal Life

In recent years, Lance focused on country music, producing the album “Country Paradise.” The project was a labor of love, involving local talents and co-produced by Lance, featuring songs that reflect his life experiences and musical evolution.

Current Endeavors

Today, Lance Woolie is celebrated not just for his musical abilities but for his inspiring life story. His latest works continue to draw from his rich life experiences, contributing to the authentic voice that defines his music.

Latest: Uber (2025)

A danceable neotraditional tune, evoking lost love with "I would give anything to see you walk through that door." Lance's striking, one-of-a-kind vocal uses no auto-tune. Uber features Brent Mason (14-time ACM Guitarist, Grammy winner, recently working with Zack Top & Post Malone), Scotty Sanders (I Never Lie- Zach Top & steel guitar for Gene Watson, Dolly Parton), and Brian Brignac (drummer for Eric Clapton, Wayne Toups). Perfect for modern "Country Gold" & '90s playlists.
