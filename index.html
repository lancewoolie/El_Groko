The Ballad of Lance

Lance Woolie, Baton Rouge-born (July 3, 1979), carved through shadows: brother's 1995 loss, addiction's grip, juvenile detention's spark with Angola Travelin’ band. Worship leader to canine unit—music as lifeline. LSU/Southern U honed his edge; Colombia birthed “Let It Ride.” Home for “Country Paradise”—grit poured into gold.

Today, Brent Mason collaborations echo bayou soul: gouge eyes for one more door-walk. Unhinged truth in every twang.

Latest: Uber (2025)

Danceable neotraditional fire: "I would give anything to see you walk through that door." No auto-tune—just Lance's one-of-a-kind vocal. Feat. Brent Mason (14x ACM, Grammy, Zack Top/Post Malone), Scotty Sanders (Zach Top steel, Gene Watson/Dolly), Brian Brignac (Eric Clapton/Wayne Toups). '90s gold for modern playlists.
